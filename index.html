About Me

I’m a former college athlete with two master’s degrees, still figuring out what I want to be when I grow up. Ironically, I studied student-athlete identity foreclosure, which is how athletes struggle to explore life beyond sports, only to fall into that exact trap myself.

After graduating from Wisconsin, I doubled down on my athletic identity and coached college wrestling for five years. I wanted to make an impact and help athletes develop beyond their sport, but as a coach, my job was to keep them locked in. Kind of a conflict of interest if you think about it. While at UTC, I also took advantage of a tuition waiver and earned an MBA. More education couldn't hurt, right?

That decision led me to my capstone course—Strategic Management with Dr. Frank Butler—where everything clicked. We competed in a business simulation, running a company that sold carbon fiber bikes. I volunteered for the CEO role, unsure what I was getting into, but ready to learn. We made plenty of mistakes and learned a lot, but ultimately, our team dominated every major performance metric. That’s when I realized what drives me: developing and executing strategies, sovling complex problems, and competing to win—the same reasons I love my sport.

So, after some difficult discussions with friends and family, I decided to step away from coaching and explore a new career path. It wasn’t just a career shift. It meant stepping away from the sport I’d dedicated my life to. That wasn’t easy... But with two master’s degrees, I assumed I’d be in high demand. Turns out, experience matters. My resume was all wrestling, no business.

Fortunately, I was able to land a role at LEO Events, where I work at the intersection of event technology, digital strategy, and analytics. I help solve complex problems, optimize processes, and make sense of messy data. It’s fast-paced, always evolving, and requires the same competitive mindset and strategic thinking I’ve relied on my whole life.

Outside of work, I’m diving into finance, strategy, sports, and web development—figuring out what sticks.

At my core, I'm driven by three things:

  • Strategy
  • Problem Solving
  • Competition
