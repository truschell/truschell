About Me

Former college athlete with two master’s degrees—still figuring out what I want to be when I grow up.

Ironically, I studied student-athlete identity foreclosure (how athletes struggle to explore careers beyond sports)… only to fall into that trap myself. I spent five years coaching college wrestling, earned an MBA at UTC, and assumed two master’s degrees would make me a hot commodity. Turns out, experience matters.

My MBA capstone—a competitive business simulation—sparked my passion for strategy, analytics, and decision-making. I stepped up as CEO (mostly because no one else wanted to), made plenty of mistakes, but ultimately led our team to dominate nearly every performance metric, including leading market share in four of five segments. That’s when I knew: I wanted to run a business.

Now, I’m building that experience. At LEO Events, I work at the intersection of event technology, digital strategy, and analytics—solving problems, optimizing processes, and making sense of complex data. Outside of work, I explore finance, strategy, analytics, and web development—figuring out what sticks.

Whatever I do, it needs to be:

  • Challenging
  • Strategic
  • Problem-Solving
